Seven ideas, one little studio

Make any photo look like it was shot for your monitor.

Wideframe knows the difference between a ratio and a real display. Everything it does — every crop, warning and export — targets the monitor you actually own, ultrawide or not.

Know your display, not just a ratio

Real presets for whatever you run — a 4K 16:9, a MacBook screen, an ultrawide G9, 5K2K, dual & triple spans. Every crop targets real pixels, so guidance reads "great on your display," not "below 5120×1440."

Frame it smart

Pan, zoom and nudge with rule-of-thirds and horizon guides — or let auto-framing find the horizon and propose the best slice. Snap to it, or take over.

See it on your desktop

A live preview drops your wallpaper behind a real menu bar, dock and icons — light or dark — so you judge the result in context before you export.

Make it livable

Subtle, optional readability tweaks — auto-darken, edge vignette, a soft scrim under the dock — so a busy image still reads as a calm desktop.

Honest about pixels

Quality guidance that teaches instead of scolds. See the exact output resolution and never ship a blurry wallpaper by accident.

Set it, keep it fresh

One-click Set as Wallpaper, per display. Then shuffle, schedule and rotate a whole collection on a timer, matched to light or dark mode.
